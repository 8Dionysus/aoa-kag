# KAG Owner Wait States

This note records what `aoa-kag` may consume now, what it may only reference,
and what must wait for stronger owner-repo growth.

Its job is to keep "not yet" explicit.

## Reading rule

When a neighboring repository owns the stronger answer, `aoa-kag` should point
to that owner surface rather than manufacturing a derived substitute.

## Wait-state map

| owner repo | `aoa-kag` may do now | `aoa-kag` must wait for | forbidden inference |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `aoa-techniques` | consume source-owned tiny exports and derive bounded lift, relation, and federation-entry surfaces | wider technique donor cohorts, richer source-owned export families, and any proof that technique lifts are good enough for broader downstream recommendation | derived metadata or relation views do not become technique meaning |
| `Tree-of-Sophia` | consume the current tiny-entry export, chunk map, retrieval-axis pack, and bounded route-local adjuncts | broader source-owned canonical exports, wider route families, and any corpus expansion beyond the current bounded Zarathustra contour | KAG route packs do not become ToS authority, root entry, or canon |
| `aoa-memo` | consume memo as a supporting bridge input and keep the memo donor registry-visible only | scar, retention, durable-consequence, live-ledger, or runtime memory-readiness activation | KAG does not own memory truth, memory readiness, or retention proof |
| `aoa-evals` | reference proof anchors and expected bounded claims for KAG-derived families | dedicated eval bundles for new KAG-specific claims, wider proof of stability, and verdict semantics for any growth gate | bounded output contracts do not count as proof |
| `aoa-playbooks` | consume scenario refs and handoff contracts as supporting inputs to derived handoff seams | new scenario-derived KAG families until a playbook-owned route is stable enough to justify them | a playbook mention does not authorize a new KAG surface by itself |
| `aoa-agents` | consume runtime artifact schemas and seam bindings as supporting inputs to reasoning handoff | actor-centric or role-centric KAG families until owner-layer role contracts demand them clearly | runtime artifact compatibility does not make KAG the runtime owner |
| `aoa-skills` | reference skills as neighboring workflow owners and future donor candidates | any skill-derived source-owned export contract or live donor posture | KAG must not infer skill meaning indirectly from playbooks, evals, or routing |
| `aoa-routing` | publish bounded consumer-facing KAG views through routing-owned entry cards | any routing-visible activation beyond owner-approved donors and declared adjuncts | routing demand alone does not justify a new KAG surface |
| `aoa-stats` | use stats only as derived observability hints for drift review or health shaping | any attempt to treat stats as source truth for KAG activation, proof, or canon | derived observability does not overrule owner truth |

## Practical rule

When a proposed KAG change feels blocked, ask:

1. Is the missing input source-owned here?
2. Is the missing claim actually proof, routing, memory, role, or scenario work?
3. Would a stronger owner export or contract make this derived surface narrower?

If the answer points outward, wait outward.
